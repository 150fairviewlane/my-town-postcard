The email currently shows "Reaching ~100,000 households per mailing" for a 4-zone territory (Marietta, Kennesaw, Smyrna, Acworth), where each zone individually mails to ~5,000 households. The correct total reach across 4 zones is **4 × 5,000 = 20,000 households**, not 100,000.

1. Find where this email pulls its household figure from. Based on the codebase, the territory record's `households` field is likely a CENSUS-DERIVED county-level estimate used for territory viability SCORING when a territory is proposed — NOT the actual mailing reach per zone. That field should NOT be used for this email's "reaching X households" line.

2. The correct calculation: count the number of mailing-area zones/cities this dealer's territory actually has (likely from `dealerTerritoriesTable` rows or the campaign-per-city structure, whichever this dealer's signup path used), and multiply by 5,000 (the fixed per-zone EDDM mailing size already used consistently elsewhere in the app — see the comment in `territories.ts`: "the frontend displays a fixed 5,000 households via USPS EDDM footer, no per-area count").

3. Fix the email to show this correctly computed total (zones × 5,000), not the unrelated census-derived `households` field. Verify against the Cobb County / Marietta-Kennesaw-Smyrna-Acworth example: 4 zones × 5,000 = 20,000 — confirm the corrected email shows this.

## Issue 2: Commission framing — keep it simple, do NOT reframe as "70% of profit"

Keep the commission line as a clear, simple percentage of the ad's sale price: "Your commission: 35% of every ad sold" (pulling from the shared `DEALER_COMMISSION_RATE` constant, not hardcoded). Do NOT change this to "70% of profit" or any profit-based framing — a dealer reading "70%" would likely anchor on that number and expect roughly double their actual payout, since "profit" implies something different to most readers than the intended 50/50 revenue-split convention behind that math. The fixed revenue percentage is the clearer, more trustworthy number for a brand-new dealer relationship, and it's also the number that's actually simple to verify against any given sale.
