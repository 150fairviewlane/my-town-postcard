Fix two problems with the city hub territory builder.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
PROBLEM 1 — TINY CITIES BEING SELECTED AS HUBS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

The Gazetteer file contains every incorporated place in the US
including tiny towns with a few hundred people. Places like
Isle of Palms SC (4,347 people) and Sullivan's Island SC (1,893
people) are being proposed as mailing hub cities. These are tiny
barrier island resort communities with almost no year-round
residents or local businesses and should never be hub cities.

FIX: Add a minimum population threshold to the candidate hub filter.

Add this constant:
  const HUB_MIN_CITY_POPULATION = 8000;

In findCandidateHubs(), filter out any city where population is
less than HUB_MIN_CITY_POPULATION before calculating catchment
households or businesses.

Also add a small exclusion set for edge cases that might slip
through despite low population counts — private gated islands
and ferry-only communities where population data is misleading:

  const RESORT_CITIES = new Set([
    "Sullivan's Island", // SC — 1,893 people, tiny barrier island
    'Isle of Palms',     // SC — 4,347 people, tiny barrier island
    'Kiawah Island',     // SC — private gated resort
    'Sea Island',        // GA — private gated resort
    'Bald Head Island',  // NC — ferry-only island, no businesses
    'Ocracoke',          // NC — tiny outer banks seasonal community
  ]);

Do NOT exclude Hilton Head Island — it has 38,158 year-round
residents, a median household income of $98,961, and a thriving
local business community. It is a legitimate and desirable
postcard market. It passes the 8,000 population minimum easily
and should be proposable as a hub city.

Do NOT exclude Myrtle Beach, Panama City Beach, or any other
large resort city with significant year-round populations and
real local business communities.

PROBLEM 2 — DOUBLE-COUNTING IN HUB CALCULATIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Household and business counts are inflated because ZIP codes
within overlapping catchment circles are counted multiple times.
Mount Pleasant SC shows 75,000 households but actually has
~39,884. Every ZIP near Mount Pleasant falls within the 15-mile
catchment radius of multiple nearby hub cities and gets counted
once for each.

FIX: After selecting the 4 hub cities, assign each nearby ZIP
to its NEAREST hub city only. Each ZIP counts toward exactly
one hub — no double counting.

Replace the current counting logic with Voronoi exclusive
assignment:

STEP 1 — Get all ZIPs within TERRITORY_SEARCH_RADIUS (40 miles)
of the dealer ZIP centroid.

STEP 2 — For each nearby ZIP, assign it to whichever hub city
centroid is closest (Haversine distance).

STEP 3 — Count households and businesses per hub using only
its exclusively assigned ZIPs:
  hub.catchmentHouseholds = sum of households for assigned ZIPs
  hub.nearbyBusinesses = sum of businesses for assigned ZIPs

STEP 4 — Re-evaluate hub qualification after exclusive assignment:
  A hub qualifies if catchmentHouseholds >= 5,000
                  AND nearbyBusinesses >= 100
  If a hub no longer qualifies, try the next best candidate.

Add a debug log after assignment:
  "Hub [city]: [N] ZIPs assigned, [H] households, [B] businesses"
This confirms the fix is working.
