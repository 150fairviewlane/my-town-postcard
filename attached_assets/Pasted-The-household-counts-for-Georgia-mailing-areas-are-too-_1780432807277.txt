The household counts for Georgia mailing areas are too low
because the catchment radius is too tight. Dealers mail to
the surrounding area — not just city limits.

Blairsville GA city limits: ~336 households
Blairsville greater area (Union County): ~11,490 households
The system is showing ~1,100 — closer to city limits than
the real catchment area dealers will actually mail to.

════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE FIX — WIDER CATCHMENT RADIUS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

In GET /api/territories/:id/mailing-areas, make these changes:

1. Increase the Voronoi assignment radius from 20 miles to 30 miles
   This captures the full county catchment area around each city

2. Change the guaranteed floor from "own ZIPs within 5 miles"
   to "own ZIPs within 15 miles" — this matches the territory
   builder's HUB_HOUSEHOLD_RADIUS and ensures small mountain
   towns like Blairsville capture their full county area

3. The Voronoi exclusive assignment still applies — each ZIP
   goes to its nearest city only — so there is no double
   counting even with the wider radius

With a 15-mile floor radius, Blairsville captures all Union
County ZIPs (which have a Blairsville mailing address) and
shows approximately 10,000-12,000 households — accurate for
what a dealer would actually mail to.

════════════════════════════════════════════════
ALSO INCREASE FOR NON-GEORGIA PROPOSALS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Apply the same 30-mile Voronoi radius to the territory builder
for non-Georgia proposals as well. The current 15-mile
HUB_HOUSEHOLD_RADIUS is too tight for rural areas.

Update in territoryBuilder.ts:
  HUB_HOUSEHOLD_RADIUS: change from 15 to 25

This means each hub city claims ZIPs within 25 miles
(exclusive Voronoi assignment), giving dealers a realistic
picture of their full mailing catchment.

════════════════════════════════════════════════
GUIDING PRINCIPLE
════════════════════════════════════════════════

When in doubt, show MORE households not fewer. Dealers are
marketing to the surrounding community that drives into town
to shop and eat — not just people who live within the city
limits. A larger household count is more accurate and makes
the territory more attractive to potential dealers.
