The postcard spot picker needs a complete visual overhaul of the sample ads. Right now every ad looks the same with a plain colored header bar and company name. I need each ad to look like a unique, professionally designed print advertisement. These ads will serve as the visual template and inspiration for the ad creation tool we are building next, so they need to look realistic and polished.
Here are the specific requirements for the redesign:
OVERALL DESIGN PRINCIPLES: Each ad must look visually distinct from every other ad. No two ads should share the same layout structure. Ads should look like they were designed by a professional graphic designer, not generated by code. Use rich backgrounds, gradients, photographic-style color treatments, and layered design elements. Think of how real local business ads look in a Valpak mailer or a glossy magazine insert. Every ad needs visual hierarchy with a clear focal point, supporting information, and a call to action.
AD CONTENT REQUIREMENTS: Large ads must include a styled logo area or icon treatment, a headline, subheadline, body copy with 2 to 3 selling points or menu items, an address, phone number, hours, and a coupon or offer box styled like a real coupon with a dashed border. Medium ads must include a logo or icon area, headline, one or two lines of body copy, phone number, address, and an offer or tagline. Small ads must include a compact but complete design with business name, phone number, one strong offer line, and a small icon or logo mark. Every ad should feel complete and ready to print, not like a placeholder.
VISUAL DESIGN REQUIREMENTS: Use full bleed background treatments. Some ads should have a dark rich background with light text like a steakhouse or upscale service. Some should have a bright white or light background with bold color accents like a pizza place or family dentist. Some should use a photographic color gradient background simulating a food or lifestyle photo. Include decorative elements like subtle textures, geometric shapes, corner accents, diagonal color splits, or bold borders that frame the content. Coupon sections should look like real coupons with scissor icons, dashed cut lines, and a distinct background color that separates them from the rest of the ad. Logo areas should be styled circles, rounded squares, or badge shapes, not just raw emoji. Use a mix of font weights and sizes to create real typographic hierarchy. The largest text in each ad should be an attention-grabbing headline or offer, not the company name.
SPECIFIC AD TREATMENTS TO USE: For Mr. Biscuits Cafe use a warm sunrise gradient background going from deep brown at the bottom to golden amber at the top, with the actual logo image displayed prominently in a circular badge, a script-style headline treatment, and a breakfast-themed layout. For the dental office use a clean white background with a deep navy and gold color scheme, a professional badge logo mark, and a welcoming headline about new patients. For the HVAC company use a deep blue to dark background with ice crystal or snowflake decorative elements, bold white text, and an emergency service badge. For the realty company use a warm cream background with forest green accents, a professional headshot placeholder area, and an elegant serif font treatment. For the insurance agency use a bold navy and gold treatment with a shield badge, trustworthy headline, and clean structured layout. For the pizza restaurant use a red and white checkered accent element, a circular logo badge on a dark red background, and an appetizing bold headline. For the lawn care company use a fresh green gradient background with a leaf or sun decorative element and outdoor lifestyle feel.
LAYOUT VARIETY REQUIREMENTS: At least two ads should use a diagonal split design where one triangle is a dark color and the other is light. At least one ad should have the logo or image on the left with text stacked on the right. At least one ad should have a full background treatment with all text overlaid on top. At least one ad should use a bold single color with white text and a contrasting coupon strip at the bottom. The Mr. Biscuits ad being the hero ad should be the most visually impressive since it is the real paying customer.
TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS: All ads are built as React components using only inline styles, no external CSS files or Tailwind classes. Each ad component receives a size prop with values of large, medium, or small and adjusts its layout and font sizes accordingly. Keep the same grid layout and spot picker functionality exactly as it is. Only replace the visual content inside each ad cell. Make sure text does not overflow or clip at any size. Use em or percentage based sizing where possible so ads scale cleanly. The overall postcard should look like something you would be proud to show a paying customer as an example of what their ad will appear alongside.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
