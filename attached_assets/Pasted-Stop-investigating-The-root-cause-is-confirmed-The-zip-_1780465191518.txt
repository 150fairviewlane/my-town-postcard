Stop investigating. The root cause is confirmed.

The zip-centroids.csv parser is reading the wrong columns
for lat/lng. The file format is:
  code, city, state, county, area_code, lat, lon
  col 0, col 1, col 2, col 3,  col 4,   col 5, col 6

The parser is likely reading col 2 and col 3 (state, county)
instead of col 5 and col 6 (lat, lon).

This means every ZIP has wrong coordinates — so no ZIPs
are found within 8 miles of any city, so business counts
return zero, so every city fails the business count check.

This is why Toccoa and Clarkesville show zero businesses
and get excluded. It is also likely affecting hub selection
for all territories and proposals.

════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE FIX
════════════════════════════════════════════════

In censusApi.ts, find where zip-centroids.csv is parsed.

Fix the column indices to read:
  zip = cols[0]
  lat = parseFloat(cols[5])
  lng = parseFloat(cols[6])

Not cols[2] and cols[3].

After fixing, rebuild the zipCentroidMap with correct
lat/lng values.
