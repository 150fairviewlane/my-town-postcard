Build an admin-only tool (not yet dealer-facing, but architected so it can be extended to dealers later) that scrapes local businesses, extracts their logos, auto-generates an ad mockup, and drafts a cold outreach email — end to end, for my review before anything sends.

## Part 2: Logo extraction

For each scraped business with a website, attempt to extract a usable logo image using this fallback chain, in order, stopping at the first success:
1. Structured data — check for an "Organization" schema (JSON-LD) on the page declaring a `logo` field
2. Open Graph image tag (`og:image`)
3. `<img>` tags in the page header with "logo" in the class name, ID, or alt text
4. Apple touch icon / high-res favicon as a last resort

If no website exists or none of the above succeed, fall back to Outscraper's `logo` field from the Google Maps result (the business's Google Business Profile photo), understanding this is lower quality/reliability.

Store the extracted image (or note that none was found) against the business record, along with which method succeeded.

## Part 3: Logo suitability filter

Before using an extracted image in an ad, run it through a two-stage filter:

Stage 1 — fast pre-filter (no AI call, reject obvious non-candidates):
- Minimum resolution threshold (reject anything too small/likely to look blurry when scaled)
- File type signal (PNG with transparency is a positive signal; plain JPG is a weaker signal)
- Reject extreme aspect ratios (very wide/short images are likely banners, not logos)

Stage 2 — vision-model judgment for anything passing Stage 1: send the image to a vision-capable model (use whichever we already have set up — GPT-4o vision or Grok vision, consistent with the QR-detection pipeline) and ask it to judge: is this a company logo suitable for placement in an advertisement? Consider whether it's a graphic/wordmark rather than a photograph, whether the background is clean or transparent, whether resolution is sufficient, and whether it plausibly represents the business by name.

Mark each business's logo status as: usable, unusable, or no-logo-found. Only businesses with a usable logo proceed to ad generation automatically; others should be flagged for manual review rather than silently skipped.

## Part 4: Auto-generate the ad

For businesses with a usable logo, feed the logo, business name, industry category, and address into our existing ad generation pipeline (the same one used for AdGenV7/SMART_HTML ad creation) to produce a mockup ad using an appropriate template for their industry. Store the generated ad image against the business record.

## Part 5: Draft the cold outreach email

For businesses with a completed ad mockup, draft a cold outreach email (don't send automatically) that:
- Attaches or embeds the ad mockup image
- Frames it clearly as a sample/preview made for them to consider (e.g. "Here's a preview of what your ad could look like on our local postcard — no obligation"), not as something already published or agreed to
- Includes basic info about My Town Postcard, the mailing area their business is in, and a clear call to action to learn more or claim the spot
- Includes a working unsubscribe/opt-out mechanism and our real business address, consistent with CAN-SPAM requirements for commercial email
- Is queued for my review, not sent automatically — I want to approve or edit each email before it goes out, at least for this first version
