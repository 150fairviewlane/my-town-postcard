POSTCARD PICKER VISUAL POLISH — DESIGN BRIEF FOR TASK #12

This is a design specification, not code. Translate every visual detail described here into the existing component using the real database spot IDs (mb, dn, re, hv, ins, pz, lw, a1, a2, hs on front; bxl, bl1, bl2, bm1, bm2, bs1, bs2 on back), the real imports from @workspace/api-client-react and ./lib/reservationStorage, and the existing PostcardScaleContext / ScaledCell scaling system. Do not change spot IDs, prices, imports, the front/back toggle, resume banner, or scaling architecture. Apply only the visual changes described below.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OVERALL POSTCARD CHROME
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The postcard container needs to feel like a real glossy printed piece, not a generic web grid.

Outer frame: white background, 1px solid #e2e8f0 border, 6px border radius, drop shadow of 0 4px 24px rgba(0,0,0,0.18) combined with 0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.1) for depth.

Header strip across the top of the postcard: dark red #7f1d1d background, height equivalent to about 28 natural pixels (will scale with the postcard). On the left in white bold text: a small mailbox emoji 📮 followed by “My Town Postcard · Front Side” (or Back Side). Font weight 800, letter-spacing 0.5. On the right in slightly transparent white (rgba 255,255,255,0.75): “Reaching 5,000 Local Homes · Summer 2026”. Font size about 9px at scale.

Footer strip across the bottom: dark navy #1a1a2e background, height about 22 natural pixels. On the left in muted white: “LOCAL POSTAL CUSTOMER · EDDM”. On the right: “PRESORTED STD · U.S. POSTAGE PAID · CLARKESVILLE GA 30523”. Both in 8px font at scale, sans-serif, letter-spacing 0.5.

Spot cells: each cell has a 0.5px outline in rgba(0,0,0,0.1) so customers can see where ads will be cropped on the printed postcard. No internal padding on the cells themselves — ad content goes edge to edge.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AVAILABLE SPOT INDICATOR
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When a spot is available for purchase it should look genuinely inviting and clearly clickable, not a generic empty box.

Default state:

- 4px dashed border in #22c55e (emerald green)
- Background rgba(240,253,244,0.95) — very pale green tint
- Centered vertical stack: large plus symbol, size label, price, dimensions
- Plus symbol color #16a34a, font weight 300 (thin), font size scales with spot size
- Size label text: “Extra Large” / “Large” / “Medium” / “Small” — bold 700, color #16a34a
- Price text: “$450” / “$350” / “$250” / “$199” — black, font weight 900, biggest text in the cell
- Dimensions text: “4 inch by 5 inch” / “4 inch by 3 inch” / “3 inch by 2 inch” / “1 inch by 2 inch” — small, gray #555

Font sizing at natural pixels (will scale down):

- XL spot: plus 80px, label 24px, price 32px, dims 16px
- L spot:  plus 60px, label 20px, price 26px, dims 14px
- M spot:  plus 42px, label 16px, price 21px, dims 12px
- S spot:  plus 28px, label 11px, price 14px, dims 9px

Hover state:

- Border color shifts to darker green #15803d
- Background shifts to rgba(22,163,74,0.15) — slightly more saturated
- Plus and label colors shift to #15803d
- Subtle 0.15s transition on all properties
- Cursor: pointer

Selected state (after click but before checkout):

- Border becomes solid 4px in amber #f59e0b
- Background rgba(254,243,199,0.95)
- Add a small “Your selection” label at the top of the cell

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RESERVED SPOT INDICATOR
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When a spot is held by another customer (status = reserved):

- Light yellow background #fefce8
- 3px dashed border in amber #fbbf24
- Centered stack: pin emoji 📌 (large, about 42px at natural scale), then “Reserved” text in dark amber #92400e, font weight 700, font size 18px at natural scale
- No hover effect, no cursor pointer (not clickable)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SAMPLE ADS — RENDER AT NATURAL SIZE, USE EXISTING SCALING
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each sample ad renders inside the existing ScaledCell wrapper at the spot’s natural pixel dimensions. The transform: scale() on ScaledCell shrinks it to fit. Use the existing INDUSTRIES data and PaidAd patterns from postcardCore — these designs describe the look, not the implementation details.

Each sample ad needs four things to feel professional:

1. A real photo (full bleed or partial) — use the industry photos already in INDUSTRIES from industryAssets.js
1. A prominent business name in a serif font (Georgia)
1. A visible offer/coupon with dashed border and brand accent color
1. A large readable phone number

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
XL SAMPLE AD (4 inch by 5 inch — natural 400 by 500 pixels)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Style: Full-bleed photo with dramatic overlay. Magazine cover energy.

Layer 1 (background): Industry photo filling entire cell, object-fit cover
Layer 2 (gradient overlay): linear-gradient from industry dark color at 80% opacity at top, fading to 20% at 35% down, back to 95% opacity at bottom — this creates readable text zones top and bottom
Layer 3 (top-right starburst): Yellow 12-point star SVG, 75 natural pixels wide, with offer text inside (like “$1 OFF” or “NEW!”). White bold text, drop shadow
Layer 4 (top-left content):

- Industry label in accent color (e.g. “PIZZA RESTAURANT”) — 11px, weight 700, letter-spacing 2, uppercase, sans-serif
- Business name in white below — 30px Georgia serif, weight 900, line height 1.05, text-shadow for legibility
  Layer 5 (center): Italic tagline in white, 24px, weight 800, italic, centered, with quotes around it
  Layer 6 (bottom):
- Coupon: dashed white border, dark transparent background, offer text in white 16px weight 900
- Address line: 📍 emoji + address, 11px white at 85% opacity
- Phone line: 📞 emoji + phone, 18px white weight 900

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COUPON COMPONENT (reusable for all ad sizes)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A reusable visual element for displaying offers. Two variants: light (for light backgrounds) and dark (for dark backgrounds).

Light variant: 2px dashed border in primary brand color. Background is the primary color at 18 alpha (very faint). 8px top/bottom padding, 14px left/right. Border-radius 6. Centered offer text in primary color, weight 900, 16px, line-height 1.

Dark variant: 2px dashed border in white. Background rgba(0,0,0,0.4). Same dimensions and padding. White centered offer text instead of primary color.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUCCESS CRITERIA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When you’re done, the picker should look like a glossy printed postcard preview where:

- Customers immediately understand they’re looking at a real 12-by-9 printed postcard
- The size hierarchy is obvious — XL clearly dominates, Small clearly smallest
- Sample ads look professional enough that customers think “that could be my ad”
- Available spots look inviting and clearly clickable
- All text is legible at any reasonable screen size
- The header/footer chrome reads as USPS-authentic
- Nothing breaks: checkout, reservations, resume banner, paid ad rendering, the scaling system all keep working
