Fix the Georgia territory mailing areas display. Three problems

════════════════════════════════════════════════
PROBLEM 1 — TOO MANY MAILING AREAS (showing 6, need max 4)
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Cap the mailing areas at 4. But do not simply truncate —
combine nearby small cities before truncating so the best
4 areas are shown.

COMBINING LOGIC:
After calculating household counts for all zone_note cities,
merge any two cities whose centroids are within 8 miles of
each other into a single mailing area:

  - Combined name: "City1 / City2"
  - Combined households: sum of both
  - Combined centroid: midpoint of both lat/lng

Apply merging greedily — start with the two closest cities,
merge them, then recalculate distances with the merged pair
and repeat until no two remaining areas are within 8 miles
of each other OR you have 4 or fewer areas.

For Franklin/Hart Counties example:
  Canon (11 hh) is 4 miles from Carnesville → merge to
    "Carnesville / Canon"
  Bowersville (11 hh) is 5 miles from Hartwell → merge to
    "Hartwell / Bowersville"
  Result: Carnesville/Canon · Hartwell/Bowersville ·
          Royston · Lavonia — exactly 4 areas

After merging, if still more than 4 areas, keep the 4 with
the highest household counts.

════════════════════════════════════════════════
PROBLEM 2 — HOUSEHOLD COUNTS TOO LOW
════════════════════════════════════════════════

The Voronoi is cutting small towns down to almost nothing
because large nearby cities steal their ZIPs.

Hartwell GA has ~4,500 residents (~1,800 households) but
shows 838 because Lavonia or Anderson SC is stealing most
of the surrounding ZIPs.

Fix the household calculation to use a minimum guaranteed
count based on the city's own population from the Gazetteer:

  guaranteedHouseholds = Math.round(city.population * 0.40)
  // 40% of population = approximate household count

  finalHouseholds = Math.max(
    voronoiHouseholds,
    guaranteedHouseholds
  )

This ensures a city with 4,500 residents shows at least
~1,800 households regardless of what the Voronoi assigns.
The Voronoi result is still used when it is higher — this
only sets a floor.

Also extend the Voronoi radius from 15 miles to 20 miles
for the Georgia mailing area calculation specifically.
Georgia's rural territories have more spread-out ZIPs and
need a wider net to capture the catchment area correctly.

PROBLEM 3 — TERRITORY TOTAL SHOWS ~22,000 BUT
           MAILING AREAS ONLY ADD UP TO ~2,500
════════════════════════════════════════════════

The territory-level household estimate (~22,000) comes from

Fix the display so the territory total shown is the SUM of
the 4 merged mailing area household counts — not the database

Update the territory detail panel:
