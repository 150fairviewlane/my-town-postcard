Two related fixes to the territory builder and mailing area display.
The core principle: err toward giving dealers MORE territory, not less.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE MODEL — TWO DIFFERENT HOUSEHOLD COUNTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

There are two distinct uses of household data:

USE 1 — TERRITORY QUALIFICATION (unique, non-overlapping)
  Used to determine if a territory is viable.
  Each household counted once across the whole territory.
  Minimum: 20,000 unique households.
  This uses the existing Voronoi exclusive assignment.

USE 2 — MAILING AREA DISPLAY (overlapping catchment radius)
  Used to show dealers and advertisers how many homes
  each mailing reaches.
  Each mailing area shows ALL households within 15 miles
  of the city center — regardless of whether those same
  households appear in an adjacent mailing area.
  A rural household between Cleveland and Helen can appear
  in both areas. That is correct — it could receive either
  mailing depending on which EDDM routes are selected.
  Minimum per area: 5,000 households.

These are different numbers serving different purposes.
Do not conflate them.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIX 1 — TERRITORY BUILDER MINIMUM HOUSEHOLDS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

In territoryBuilder.ts, update the minimum threshold:

  const MIN_TERRITORY_HOUSEHOLDS = 20000;

After Voronoi assignment, calculate total unique territory
households (sum of exclusive Voronoi-assigned households
across all hubs — no double counting).

If total unique households < 20,000:
  Continue bundling neighboring counties until >= 20,000
  Use the same neighbor bundling logic already in place
  Apply the same 75-mile distance cap
  Same state only

Only stop bundling when BOTH conditions are met:
  unique households >= 20,000
  AND business count >= MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT

If after MAX_NEIGHBOR_RINGS rings still under 20,000:
  Return proposal with isViable = false
  viabilityMessage = 'This area may not have enough
  households for 4 full postcard mailings of 5,000 each.
  Contact us to discuss options.'

Remove or update any previous MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT-only
stopping condition — household minimum is now equally
important.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIX 2 — MAILING AREA DISPLAY USES RADIUS NOT VORONOI
════════════════════════════════════════════════

In GET /api/territories/:id/mailing-areas, change the
household calculation for display purposes:

CURRENT (wrong for display):
  Voronoi exclusive assignment — each ZIP goes to nearest
  city only. Small cities get very few ZIPs.

NEW (correct for display):
  For each mailing area city, count ALL households within
  15 miles of that city centroid regardless of whether
  those households are also within 15 miles of another city.

  mailingAreaHouseholds = sum of zip.households for all
    ZIPs where distance(zip.centroid, city.centroid) <= 15 miles

This is a simple radius sum — no Voronoi, no exclusivity.

With this approach:
  Blairsville captures all Union County ZIPs within 15 miles
    → approximately 10,000-12,000 households
  Hiawassee captures all Towns County ZIPs within 15 miles
    → approximately 5,000-7,000 households
  Clayton captures all Rabun County ZIPs within 15 miles
    → approximately 15,000-18,000 households

Some households appear in multiple areas. That is correct
and expected — they are in the catchment zone of both cities
and could receive either mailing.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIX 3 — MERGE MAILING AREAS BELOW 5,000 HOUSEHOLDS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

After calculating radius-based household counts for each city:

If any mailing area has fewer than 5,000 households:
  Find the nearest other mailing area (by city centroid distance)
  Merge them:
    Combined name: "City1 / City2"
    Combined households: larger of the two radius counts
      (not sum — they overlap, so use the larger one as
      representative of the combined area)
    Combined centroid: midpoint

Keep merging until all areas have >= 5,000 households
or only 1 area remains.

After merging, cap at 4 areas maximum.
If more than 4 areas all have >= 5,000 households,
keep the 4 with the highest household counts.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIX 4 — ALSO APPLY TO NON-GEORGIA PROPOSALS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

The same mailing area display logic (radius-based, not Voronoi,
5,000 minimum, 4 maximum) should apply to auto-generated
territory proposals for all states.

Update buildCityHubProposal() to calculate display households
using the radius approach rather than exclusive Voronoi counts.

Keep Voronoi for QUALIFICATION (determining which hubs to select)
but use radius for DISPLAY (showing household counts to dealers).
