Two fixes needed. ZIP 22901 (Charlottesville VA) is generating a
territory that only supports one postcard mailing. Every territory
must support at least 4 mailing cards. Also, Virginia independent
cities are being missed by the county lookup.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE CORE PROBLEM — SIZING FOR 4 CARDS NOT 1
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each postcard card has 15 ad slots. A dealer needs to sell 60 total
slots across 4 cards to complete a full mailing cycle. At a
realistic 15-25% close rate on business outreach, the dealer needs:

  60 slots ÷ 20% close rate = 300 businesses minimum to approach
  But pipeline gets depleted — businesses that already bought
  won't buy again immediately. So you need fresh prospects.
  A healthy territory needs 400-600 businesses to comfortably
  support 4 cards with pipeline to spare.

The current MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT of 300 is sized for 1 card.
It needs to be sized for 4 cards with pipeline.


════════════════════════════════════════════════
FIX 1 — UPDATE TERRITORY SIZE CONSTANTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Update these constants in territoryBuilder.ts:

  // Minimum for a 4-card viable territory with healthy pipeline
  const MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT = 400;

  // Target business count — ideal territory size
  // Builder keeps bundling until it hits this target even if
  // MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT is already reached
  const TARGET_BUSINESS_COUNT = 600;

  // Only stop bundling early (at MIN) if adding more counties
  // would exceed the distance cap or cross state lines
  const MAX_BUSINESS_COUNT = 2000;

  // Max bundle radius — keep at 75 miles
  const MAX_BUNDLE_RADIUS_MILES = 75;

CHANGE THE BUNDLING LOGIC:

Current behavior: bundle neighbors until totalCount >= MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT
  then stop.

New behavior: bundle neighbors until totalCount >= TARGET_BUSINESS_COUNT
  (600), but accept the territory if totalCount >= MIN_BUSINESS_COUNT
  (400) and no more neighbors are available within the radius or
  same state.

This means the builder keeps adding neighboring counties even after
reaching 400 businesses, continuing until it hits 600 or runs out
of viable neighbors. The dealer gets a larger, more comfortable
territory rather than the minimum viable one.

Example for Charlottesville:
  Albemarle County alone: ~500 businesses → currently stops here
  With new logic: continues bundling
  Add Fluvanna County (~150 businesses) → 650 total → stops, target met
  Territory name: "Charlottesville / Fluvanna Area"
  Dealer now has comfortable pipeline for 4+ cards

FIX 2 — VIRGINIA INDEPENDENT CITIES
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Virginia has 38 independent cities that exist outside county
boundaries in the Census data. They have their own FIPS codes
and their own business count data in county-business-patterns.txt.

When the territory builder processes a Virginia ZIP code, it looks
up the county and bundles neighboring counties — but it misses
adjacent independent cities entirely because they don't appear
in the county adjacency file as counties.

Key Virginia independent city FIPS codes (state FIPS 51):
  Charlottesville city: 51540
  Richmond city:        51760
  Virginia Beach city:  51810
  Norfolk city:         51710
  Chesapeake city:      51550
  Newport News city:    51700
  Hampton city:         51650
  Alexandria city:      51510
  Roanoke city:         51770
  Lynchburg city:       51680
  Harrisonburg city:    51660
  Fredericksburg city:  51630

Fix the bundling logic for Virginia (stateFips = '51'):

When building a territory in Virginia, after getting the initial
county's neighbors from the adjacency file, also check whether any
Virginia independent city centroid is within MAX_BUNDLE_RADIUS_MILES
of the territory's centroid.

Use the county-centroids.csv file — independent cities appear in
it with their own GEOID (5-digit FIPS). Their STATEFP is '51' and
COUNTYFP is their 3-digit city FIPS.

If a Virginia independent city centroid is within 75 miles AND
the city has businesses in county-business-patterns.txt:
  Include it as a bundling candidate alongside neighboring counties
  Add its business count to the territory total
  Include its name in the city list

For ZIP 22901 specifically:
  Albemarle County (51003) neighbors include Charlottesville city
  (51540) which is literally surrounded by Albemarle County.
  The system must include Charlottesville city in any Albemarle
  County territory.

This same fix applies to all Virginia ZIPs near independent cities.
The most important pairings:
  Albemarle + Charlottesville city
  Fairfax County + Alexandria city + Falls Church city
  Roanoke County + Roanoke city + Salem city
  Pittsylvania County + Danville city
  Augusta County + Staunton city + Waynesboro city
  Rockingham County + Harrisonburg city
  Spotsylvania + Fredericksburg city

FIX 3 — OTHER STATES WITH INDEPENDENT CITIES
════════════════════════════════════════════════

Virginia is the most extreme case but a few other states have
similar independent jurisdictions:

Maryland: Baltimore city (FIPS 24510) is independent of
  Baltimore County (24005). Always bundle them together.

Missouri: St. Louis city (FIPS 29510) is independent of
  St. Louis County (29189). Always bundle them together.

Nevada: Carson City (FIPS 32510) is an independent city.
  Bundle with Douglas or Ormsby area.

Add these specific overrides to the bundling logic:
  If starting county is Baltimore County MD (24005):
    Always include Baltimore city (24510) in the bundle
  If starting county is St. Louis County MO (29189):
    Always include St. Louis city (29510) in the bundle
  If starting county is Baltimore city MD (24510):
    Always include Baltimore County (24005) in the bundle
  If starting county is St. Louis city MO (29510):
    Always include St. Louis County (29189) in the bundle
